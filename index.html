    Course Description

    This seminar focuses on experimental practices in philosophy, art, and technoscience. This year's course critically introduces some vital interdisciplinary discourses in the humanities and arts concerning subjectivation/subjectification, process, and performance by threading a narrative from the modern crisis of representation to materialist notions of distributed agency and affect. The seminar supports and is informed by creation-research approaching these questions sensitive to conditions framing ethico-aesthetics.

    We begin with Guattari's turn from psychotherapy to a speculative mode of art, and Wittgenstein's more caustic critique of theories of language, logic, and representation. We then discuss the broader 20c turn from representation to performance and embodiment, reading for example, Artaud's challenge to theater as "dramatic literature," and Maturana and Varela's extension from the living to the autopoietic. These implicate critiques of technology and science which we examine in the context of media art and experimental performance. In the latter part of the course, we discuss alternative approaches to subjectivity and experience, introducing phenomenology, philosophies of process (Heraclitus, Stengers, Whitehead, Laozi, Zhuangzi), and material topological dynamical systems.

    The seminar will be centered on close readings and discussions of selected texts, with opportunities for presenting installation or performative work. Students will prepare an in-class presentation during the term, and a 15 page paper at the end of the semester.

    This course is open to Graduate students in any of the AME concentrations as well as MAS PhD students. MAS PhD students will be able to use this course on their Program of Study for one of the AME required courses. Faculty from AME and English will also be involved in the Seminar.

    This class also is open to graduate students in English and will count as a theory course. If you are an English graduate student, please make sure you have signed up for the class under the ENG prefix.

    1. Critical studies of media arts and sciences

    What are art practice, art research, art as vehicle, and creation research?

    Readings Notes
    Chasmosis, Chapters 1 - 2, Guattari

    2. Production of subjectivity

    What could be alternative conditions for the production of subjectivity?

    Readings Notes
    Chasmosis, Chapters 6 - 7, Guattari

    3. Representation

    Meaning vs. information vs. semiotics. What's (not) a representation? What's an object and predicate? What's alphabet, grammar, syntax; graph?

    Readings Notes
    Philosophical Investigations, Pages 1-100, 192-212, selections from 1-309 (notes), Wittgenstein

    4. The Study of Experience. Phenomenology contra psychology.

    Problems with psychologism, biological reductionism, and category. A material phenomenology, tacit knowledge and substrates of experience.

    Readings Notes
    Kusch: Husserl's critique of Brentano's psychologism David Woodruff Smith on Husserl's method of transcendental reduction Gendlin on felt meaning William James, Essays on Radical Empiricism. "How Two Minds Can Know One Thing," p. 123-137.

    5. Process and Transformation

    Figures and philosophies of process.

    Readings Notes
    Maturana and Varela. Autopoiesis, The Organizatrion of the Living, pp. 59 to 143. Chapters 1-3 ( notes), Chapters 4 -5, Appendix Lefebvre Rhythmanalysis; Prigogine, Stengers Whitehead Process and Reality Heraclitis Laozi (Tao) and Chuangzi

    6. Material Topology

    From body without organs to materiality without objects (things). Continuity, open / closed sets, limit, topological spaces, homeomorphism; the qualitative and the anexact sans metric, sans number.

    Readings Notes
    Janich, introduction to topology. Badiou: mathematics is ontology; Stengers on mathematics as poetic ontology.

    7. Material Phenomenology

    What's the virtual, actual, tangible, physical, real, impossible, imaginary? Consciousness, intentionality, the problem of intersubjectivity.

    Readings Notes
    David Woodruff Smith, Husserl and Intentionality 93-104 Selections from Merleau-Ponty, Phenomenology of Perception (tr. D. Landes): Space, 281-344 Temporality 469-495 (paginations in French edition) Heidegger, "What Is a Thing?" Petitot, "Morphological Eidetics for Phenomenology of Perception." Husserl Ideen §27-40 from the chapters on bracketing and epoche, and on intentional consciousness Meleau-Ponty, The Spatiality of One’s own Body and Motility 114-172

    8. Performance

    Experimental performance vs. spectacle. Art as vehicle. Everyday, unmarked vs marked gesture.

    Readings Notes
    Debord, Society of Spectacle (ch 1) Artaud: ch. 8: Theater of Cruelty Artaud: ch 9: The Theater of Cruelty, Second Manifesto Derrida: "La parole soufflée" and "The Theater of Cruelty and the Closure of Representation" Grotowski pp. 0-59 , pp. 116-125, pp. 254-262 Sponge ECART Barbara Formis Badiou on event

    9. Subjectivation and art

    What makes something "live," or interactive, vs responsive? concurrent? What is art research? Why should artists make things of common concern?

    Readings Notes
    Case Study: Latour and Weibel: Making Things Public ZKM 2005 Guattari, last chapter Alan Milchman and Alan Rosenberg, “The Final Foucault: Government of Others and Government of Oneself” (Cambridge Scholars Publishing, 2008)

    10. Implications

    What is humanities in a field, plenist, processual mode? What could be rigorously experimental, humane but not anthropocentric approach to physics, ecology, architecture, economics, ethics, engineering and design?

    Readings Notes
    Stengers "Conversation," "Sixth Day" Goethe, The Metamorphosis of Plants, tr. Miller (MIT) Marder, Plant-Thinking: A Philosophy of Vegetal Life (Columbia)
